Hang

Mobile & Web UI/UX Case Study (in progress)

Abstract

Hang is a mobile application project that focuses on helping people build genuine, platonic relationships with those with similar interests near the user. Hang is designed to disrupt modern social media/ dating platforms in order to resolve some social problems while also collaborating with local businesses.

Discover

Redesign

Stakeholders came to me with a basic sketch of their idea and a list of preferred functions:

Target Audience

The primary target audience of this mobile application are young adults who are willing to spend time outside of their homes (user research done through 1-on-1 interviews)

User Profiles

Market Competition

We have analyzed Tinder, Bumble, Hinge and Tantan and how they address platonic relationships. 

Problem Statement

Young adults need a new, spontaneous way of meeting people around their area in order to safely make platonic friendships. 

Ideate

Proposed Solution

Many iterations have been made since the start of the project. In order to make a more user-friendly experience, we've decided to incorporate Google's material design in order to adhere to modern design trends. 

In order to make the app more spontaneous, we've designed it so that users will be able to see the essential details of an event (through user research) in the home screen, as well as navigate to specific categories they are looking for. Users also have the ability to create their own "hang" with a simple click of a button if they don't see events they are interested in.

In regards to safety and privacy issues, we've implemented a system where users will be required to take a temporary selfie in order to identify themselves to the group of people who are going to the event. 

Prototype

Early Prototypes

In order to make the user-journey effortless, I tried to make the process of registering for a hang very quick. I tried to hide the information in an expandable so that the main screen doesn't feel too cluttered and the user doesn't feel overwhelmed with text/ information.

The expanding tabs displays more details of the "hang". The expandable tab shows people who RSVP’d. The user can also press RSVP on the expanded version of the tab, but not on the closed form in order to suggest the user to not cancel. 

Additional Features

During development and ideation, we realized we wanted to add more features to the app so that users can create a virtual/ social community within the app. Because of the additional features, it was necessary to take a step back and reorganize the information architecture. That's why we added bottom nav bar in order to organize the information and features.

Hangs - The main page which feature random hangs created by other users. The hangs can also be filtered based on preferences.

Categories - The category page is similar to that of the main hang page, but allows users to browse through categories they are specifically interested in.

Sign Up / Log In

Made the long sign-up process more enjoyable through animated transitions and perceived affordance.

Create a Hang

Allows users to create a hang in 4 easy screens

Friends

Allows users to build friendships and message people they've met in events. There are also suggestions where users can add people they've met in past hang events.

Profile

Allows the user to keep track of how many hangs they've been to and their analytics. The hang they are currently RSVP'd to is also there for easy access.

Hawker Sensor

Mobile UI/UX Case Study

Abstract

Hawker Sensor is a case study that utilizes the data set of Hawker centers, public food courts, in Singapore from the National Environment Agency. I have analyzed two sources for this case study, and conceptualized a location based mobile application that allows the selected target audience to keep track of their culinary experience in Singaporean hawker centers.

Discover

Rationale

The rationale behind choosing this data set is that as an exchange student from Illinois, United States of America, I wanted to explore the different dining experiences that hawker centers in Singapore offer. I also wanted to utilize my experience and the experience of my fellow exchange students to create an application that would make the dining experience in Singapore more enjoyable and exciting. As an iconic aspect of Singaporean culture, I believed that studying hawker centers would be an interesting opportunity to incorporate this new cultural experience into my academic interests. 

Target Audience

The primary target audience of this mobile application are exchange students and travelers. The secondary target audience are locals; however, through my interviews I have discovered that most locals go to the nearest hawker centers and also usually eat from home. I have also decided to focus primarily on young adults because of their low-budget style of living. I believe that  young adults and college students will take advantage of the cheaper meals offered at hawker centers. 

User Profiles

Market Competition

I have analyzed Yelp and Grab as the primarily competitors. Although there is no application that focuses solely on hawker centres, I have analyzed these two food-centric apps in order to make an application that is familiar with the target audience. 

Problem Statement

Foreigners who are not familiar with Singapore and Singaporean culture need an efficient way of exploring new local cuisines and keeping track of their culinary experiences. 

https://data.gov.sg/dataset/hawker-centres_new

Ideate

Proposed Solution

The function is this application will be useful for the selected target audience because it will allow the target audience to utilize different functions without having to switch over to another application. Hawker Sensor aims to incorporate the navigation feature inside the application so that the user can easily navigate to different hawker centers while simultaneously looking at the different stalls/ dishes, and then ranking them afterwards. This application aims to make the user flow between those three stages of an exchanger’s/ travelers culinary journey (deciding where to eat, deciding what to eat, and reviewing if it was good) into one seamless process.  

Application Features

Home - The Home page will be where the user first lands. This page would include a map that takes up most of the screen in order to let the user visualize the distance, and utilize the map for directions to the desired hawker center. The main function of the page will be to identify which hawker centre the user wants to visit and they will be able to filter the hawker centres through distance, reviews, and number of reviews, which were indicated as important factors that the target audience thought about when choosing where to eat. 

Explore - The Explore page will allow users to explore different types of dishes when they are unsure of what to eat. There will be a random function/  button that the user can utilize when they are having a difficult time choosing what to eat, and view an assortment of recommended dishes. 

Community - The Community page will allow users to see what the community (users of this application) ranks as the best food and where to eat them. The food will be ranked through the compilation of individual user rankings.  

Personal - The Personal page will act as a diary/log for the user, allowing the user to rank the dishes that they’ve tried and keep track of all the hawker centers that they have visited. The user is able to mark what dish/ hawker center the user have tried/ visited, which is then automatically added into the personal page.

Prototype

Lo-Fidelity / Sketching

While developing some quick hand drawn sketches, I wanted the user to utilize the map as a visual tool in order to understand the distance between the different hawker centers and them. Thus, the home screen was drawn with a map that covers most of the screen. 

Mid-Fidelity 

In the mid-fidelity prototype, I’ve just a monochromatic color scheme with the addition of white and black. I’ve also used the #D03838 red instead of the actual red in the Singaporean flag because it was too saturated and caused strain to the user’s eye. I’ve also incorporated red circles as the rating system rather than the stars in order to refer to Singapore’s nickname, little red dot. I’ve also reduced the amount of circles in “recently visited” as well as categorized the dishes so that it does not overwhelm the user.
